Professional Background

I am Guta Demisu Kebede a passionate Lecturer and Researcher, igniting minds in the Department of Mathematics at Dilla University, Ethiopia. My career is driven by an irresistible desire to fuse high-level theory with hands-on computational mastery transforming mathematics from abstract beauty into real-world power. Every lecture I give is an invitation to enter the vibrant frontier where ideas spark and innovation thrives.

In my research realm, I thrive on the sexy complexities of Numerical Analysis, especially singularly perturbed problems. I carve out groundbreaking, higher-order computational methods to unravel the mysteries of reaction-diffusion systems and time-delay dynamics. My mathematical arsenal? The Crank-Nicolson method, B-spline collocation, and bold, fitted mesh schemes each chosen to break through where bland, conventional techniques simply can’t compete.

Far beyond the lecture hall, I showcase my work in top-tier journals like Results in Applied Mathematics and Frontiers in Applied Mathematics and Statistics. As a global research collaborator, I’m relentlessly remixing and refining numerical schemes to deliver unmatched accuracy and computational stamina-well-equipped for tomorrow’s most tantalizing scientific puzzles. Join me on this thrilling journey, where mathematics is never bland and the next breakthrough is always just one bold algorithm away.
